Definitions
  • Average Utility Rate*: The utility rate (per kilowatt hour) paid by the average customer of your utility company.
  • Savings*: The difference between your Utility Bill* and your Vivint Solar Bill.
  • Vivint Solar Bill: Your Vivint Solar Bill based on the Actual Production from the PV System of the solar energy system at your home and your contract rate during that month.
  • Utility Bill*: This value is calculated by multiplying your Average Utility Rate* and your Actual Production from the PV System. This is not necessarily what you paid your utility company, but what you could have had to pay your utility company for receiving the same amount of power generated by the PV system from the grid.
  • Actual Production from the PV System: The amount of energy produced by the solar energy system at your home.
Footnote * Values are representative of the average customer of your Utility Company.

The savings numbers provided above are just a ballpark estimate based on the utility costs in your area and the costs associated with your particular solar system. Actual savings will vary based ona a variety of factors, including (without limitation): system size, your home's location, position of you solar panels, insolation (shading on your solar panels), energy consumption patterns, utility rates, financing charges, etc.

ASSUMPTIONS AND DISCLAIMERS

These estimates are based on the following assumptions:

  • The rate that you pay for energy from the utility company is the average blended rate for the average customer in your utility for the past 12 months. This blended rate is calculated by taking the energy usage of the average customer and applying it to the rate tariffs in your utility which may include tiered and Time of Use (TOU) pricing structures. In order to arrive at the "average customer" for your particular utility, we use the average consumption profile for your utility, as provided by the US Energy Information Administration (EIA).
  • Your total usage would not have changed if you did not have the solar energy system installed and all the power that you would have used would have been purchased from the utility at the blended rate indentified.
  • These calculations assume that the amount of energy produced by your solar energy system does not exceed your total energy consumption during the applicable time period.

Disclaimers

1 Estimates are not a guarantee of energy production, cost savings, and/or the existence or nature of any net metering or incentive program. Utility rates are subject to change, and such changes cannot be accurately predicted

2 This estimate does not take into consideration any net metering programs, or any other incentive programs.
