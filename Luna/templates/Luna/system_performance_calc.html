Definitions
  • Design Estimate w/Degradation: Over time, the solar cells within solar panels become less efficient. This number takes the initial Design Estimate you were given before receiving PTO and adjusts it down to account for the efficiency loss over time.
  • Weather Corrected Estimate: Solar energy systems (like the one on your home) are very sensitive to changes in the weather. This number takes your Design Estimate w/Degradation and factors it up or down depending on how clear or cloudy the weather was in your area for that month.
  • Actual Production: The amount of energy produced by the solar energy system at your home.
  • Designed Performance Ratio w/Degradation: This percentage represents how well the solar energy system at your home produced compared to the Design Estimate w/Degradation. It is calculated by dividing the Actual Production by the Design Estimate w/Degradation.
  • Weather Corrected Performance Ratio: This percentage represents how well the solar energy system at your home produced compared to the Weather Corrected Estimate. It is calculated by dividing the Actual Production by the Weather Corrected Estimate.
